About Me

I am a computer scientist and researcher in machine learning, computational linguistics and computational social sciences. In my career in the industry, I worked on problems including language modeling and understanting, topic modeling and supervised/ unsupervised learning in the private sector. I also did some consulting work in data architecture and analytics, data science, and program management in the public and non-profit sectors.

I completed my masters in Information Mangement at University of Maryland, College Park and I have a bachelor in engineering in Computer Science from Damascus University.

Research Interests

My focus is in the applications of natural language processing and machine learning in computational social sciences. I’m interested in understanding how to unpack information encoded in our language and better understand people’s behavior from their communication and interactions with other humans as well as technology and AI. I am particularly captivated by: 1) how people across different cultures and socioeconomic groups form and update their opinions and beliefs; 2) in what ways people’s intuitions and assumptions about the world shape the way they behave and consume information; and 3) the mechanisms of interaction and social membership through which people are influenced by or influence others.

Research Areas:

  • Opinion formation, News consumption
  • Psycholinguistics
  • Universal Language Representation, Multi-task learning
